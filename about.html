Things To Do Outdoors

Hiking

Hiking is when you go out into nature and walk along a trail to end up at a destination of your choosing. It is different than walking because walking is when you go out and walk anywhere that isnt in nature. Typically hiking is done on dirt trails and includes obstacles such as small rocks or streams that you must pass over.

Backpacking

Backpacking is similar to hiking except you plan on staying the night outdoors. Backpackers bring a large pack filled with all of their essentials and hike to where they will stay. Backpackers usually sleep in a tent or a hammock and pack as light as they can since every pound of equipment makes the hike more challenging. Backpackers usually eat freeze dried meals that they prepare with hot water to save weight.

Camping

Camping is more of a broad term for going outside and staying the night in nature. It can include sleeping in a tent, a hammock, a car, a motorhome, an RV, or even plopping your sleeping back directly on the ground. The main requirement to be considered camping is that it is outdoors and outside of the city.

"Glamping"

"Glamping" is what elitist call the activity of going out into the wilderness with an RV, travel trailer, or car and setting up camp. Usually this is defined by finding a campsite that has bathrooms and power hookups for devices such as TVs. Do not let others make you feel bad for camping this way as it is a perfectly valid way to get out and enjoy nature!
